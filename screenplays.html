The Ostritch

Coming of age · Short · 1 current draft

Draft 1

On the last day of school, Aaron, Elliot, Owen, and the rest of the group gather for one final summer party — not yet understanding it might be the last time everything feels this easy. As the night moves from a house party to the pier at sunset, news of Owen joining the army and Olivia leaving for London begins to land. A coming-of-age short about the moment before everything changes.

Draft progression

Draft 1
Draft 1 — Jun 2026 First pass. Getting the story on paper. There is a clear start and finish however more needs to take place in the middle.

The Spiral

Drama · Short · 1 current draft

Draft 1

Katie ends things with Jack — she's proud of what he's doing but can't keep doing it alongside him. What follows is Jack unravelling in real time: the phone calls, the drinking, the bravado, the metro at night with a can in his hand. A short about the gap between holding it together and falling apart, and how quickly one becomes the other.

Draft progression

Draft 1
Draft 1 — Jun 2026 First pass. Jack's voice is loud and present — maybe too loud. The breakup scene is doing a lot of heavy lifting early on. The cuts between scenes are working well rhythmically; need to make sure they keep that energy through to the end.
INT. JACK'S BEDROOM JACK and KATIE sit at the edge of the bed. KATIE I'm sorry. I'm proud of you for everything you're doing but I just can't go along with it anymore. JACK Get the fuck out then. Why are you still here. CUT. Jack is still sat at the edge of the bed. We hear a door slam downstairs. He looks at his phone. Throws it off the wall. JACK Fuck! CUT. Jack standing. On the phone now. JACK Yeah we broke up. Yes I'm fine. I'll be there in a minute — just make sure you bring it. He gets ready. Drinks as he does. Music blasting. JACK I'll show her what she's missing. CUT. EXT. STREET - NIGHT Jack walks to the metro station, can in hand. Two girls pass. He shouts something. They laugh and keep walking. JACK (to himself) They were ugly anyway. He crushes the can. Throws it at the wall. Laughs.

Ordinary Lives

Drama · TV Episode · 1 current draft

Draft 1

Episode one opens on the morning of Elliot and Isobel's wedding — Aaron, Elliot, Owen, and Zach hungover in a hotel room with ninety minutes to get there. An ensemble drama about a group of friends who fear they're only going to see each other at moments like this one. Running through it: Aaron and Olivia, circling each other across every room they've ever been in together.

Draft progression

Draft 1
Draft 1 — Jun 2026 First pass. The hotel room opening works well — drops you straight in. Aaron and Olivia's dynamic is the engine of the episode; need to make sure it doesn't get lost in the ensemble. The pier ending landed well in the read-through.
INT. HOTEL ROOM - DAY AARON wakes up in a hotel bed. A woman he doesn't recognise is asleep next to him. He shrugs. Checks his phone. AARON Shit. He finds OWEN and ZACH passed out on the sofa. AARON (CONT'D) You guys need to wake up. ZACH What? AARON We have to be there in an hour and a half. They call ELLIOT. A phone buzzes from the bathroom. They find him passed out in the bathtub, the shower curtain pulled down. OWEN Found him. ZACH It stinks in here. AARON Elliot! Wake up. We're gonna be late. ELLIOT (confused) What time is it? AARON Ten. ELLIOT You're joking. AARON No man. Come on. Get ready.
