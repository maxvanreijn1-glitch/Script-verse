The Spiral

Drama · Short · 1 current draft

Draft 1

RYAN is two years clean when his girlfriend leaves him. What follows is not a relapse — not immediately — but the slow gravitational pull back towards everything he thought he'd escaped. A portrait of recovery as an ongoing act of will, and what happens when the thing holding you together walks out the door.

Draft progression

Draft 1
Draft 1 — Jun 2026 First pass. Ryan's voice is coming through clearly. The challenge is pacing the spiral — it needs to feel gradual and inevitable at the same time. The breakup scene needs work; right now it's doing too much explaining.
INT. RYAN'S FLAT - KITCHEN - MORNING The flat is tidy. Aggressively tidy. The kind of clean that takes effort every single day. RYAN (29) stands at the counter eating cereal, standing up. He doesn't sit down for meals anymore. Sitting down feels like staying. His phone buzzes. He looks at it. He puts it face-down. He finishes his cereal. Washes the bowl. Dries it. Puts it away. Goes to work. CUT TO: INT. RYAN'S FLAT - KITCHEN - NIGHT (THREE WEEKS LATER) The flat is not tidy.

Ordinary Lives

Drama · Feature · 1 current draft

Draft 1

A group of old friends keep finding themselves back in the same room — weddings, funerals, thirtieth birthdays — and slowly realising they only exist to each other now in the margins of real events. Running through the reunions is a quieter story: two of them, JAMES and CLAIRE, circling something neither of them has ever quite said out loud.

Draft progression

Draft 1
Draft 1 — Jun 2026 First pass. The ensemble structure is working — each reunion feels distinct. James and Claire's storyline needs more room to breathe; at the moment the group scenes are crowding it out. The ending is a placeholder.
INT. FUNCTION ROOM - EVENING A wedding. The kind where the speeches ran long and the food arrived cold and nobody minded. The dancefloor is half-full. The bar is fully occupied. JAMES (32) stands at the edge of the room with a drink he hasn't touched, watching the group he's known since he was seventeen. They're louder than they used to be. Or maybe he's quieter. CLAIRE (31) appears at his shoulder. CLAIRE You've got your thinking face on. JAMES I don't have a thinking face. CLAIRE You've had it since we were sixteen. It hasn't improved. A beat. They watch the dancefloor together. JAMES When did this become the only time we see each other? Claire doesn't answer straight away. She knew this conversation was coming. It comes at every wedding. CLAIRE Probably around the time life got in the way. JAMES Right. Another beat. JAMES (date: 'Jun 2026') And when did we decide to let it?
